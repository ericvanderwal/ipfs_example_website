Community

Title: Playmaker Social Community
Venue: Slack
Description: Creating, managing, and interacting with the Playmaker social community on Slack has been an absolute privilege and at the top of my list for successes in developer relations. The regular (and less regular) community members have developed unique projects from their hard work and imagination; From Square Enix to teens developing indie games for school. It is an open and inclusive environment that welcomes new members enthusiastically.

Community Feedback:

The Playmaker Slack channel has been an invaluable resource on my game development path. It has been both a solid community for sharing and bouncing ideas off of, as well as a repository of knowledge that is easy to tap into.

The sense of community is immediate – as long as I’ve been a member, each and every new person who joins is greeted with a handwritten welcome (no bots here!) and members quickly take that warmness and carry on the tradition and welcome newer folks in a similar manner.

The community is also amazingly helpful; whether its puzzling out how to solve a problem (people will look through an issue step by step to help out!) or give feedback on each others work – its one of the best places I’ve been for game dev assistance.

- Thomas Kildren

The Playmaker Slack community was definitely one of my inspirations for becoming a Unity Certified Instructor and developer. The fact that we were tackling hard problems together, and being able to discuss the ins and outs of Playmaker with Eric really made this community valuable.

Eric was the main facilitator of the community needs and Playmaker, hearing our issues and needs for additional scripts, actions, and guidance. I could always count on Playmaker, the Ecosystem tool, or community members to share their code snippets and solutions for my project. I never felt frustrated and overwhelmed.

One of the best things is the yearly game jam led by Eric. The event encourages me to have a weekend stretch goal to finish developing a (small) game that will be played by the community. This is usually the pinnacle of the community events and we have a great time learning to know each other and work together.

- Maor Bluman

Game Jams (Hack-A-Thons)

Title: Playmaker Game Jam 2018 - 2020
Venue: Itch.io (Online)
Description:

I created the Playmaker Game Jam (hack-a-thon), an annual itch.io game jam, open to all Playmaker users. It was first started to help foster community between Playmaker customers ( a visual scripting solution for the Unity Game Engine) and has been held annually since 2018.

The Game Jam became a rallying point for many developers as a place to expand their creative horizons outside of a commercial environment and to interact with their peers. It is an event that I still look forward to each year.

The three-day event was customarily held online in the summer or fall, with teams battling to create the most entertaining games from scratch. As the sole organizer, I was responsible for all planning and operations. Tasks included scheduling, sponsorships deals (prizes), rules and regulations, judging criteria, and community management.

Venue: Playmaker Visual Scripting Tool for Unity
Description: VRTK (Virtual Reality Toolkit) was the premier VR system for the Unity Game Engine. During my time with Playmaker, I developed numerous integrations between third-party packages and Playmaker using C# that were released open-source. While the code quality is rought around the edges (I was new at the time), it functions well and has been used in places ranging from the Smithsonian to early AAA game prototypes.

Symposium

Title: Integrative Fertility Symposium 2015 - 2019
Venue: Vancouver, Canada
Description:

The Integrative Fertility Symposium (IFS) was a natural evolution from my work with Healthy Seminars, where I planned content, coached speakers, and organized technical aspects for over 150 live webinars and in-person events.

For the IFS, I was the co-organizer for five three and half-day symposiums from 2015 to 2019. The symposium hosted over 250 physical attendees, typically 20 - 35 international speakers, in three simultaneous talks while broadcasting live to around an additional 200 - 300 customers.

Planning started six months before the event each year, and my role included everything from the top-level planning to making sure attendees had their guest passes. Some notable contributions were speaker selection, schedule planning, developing the event website (booking, payment, permissioned content access), overseeing all technical aspects of the recording and live streaming, reviewing talks, and more.
